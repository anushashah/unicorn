Management And National Park Location

Ayubia National Park is strategically positioned in the Himalayan foothills, providing breathtaking views of the surrounding mountains and valleys.

Administration: It encompasses several hill stations, including Ayubia, Khanspur, Khairagali, and Changla Gali, each offering its own unique charm and natural beauty.

Conservation Efforts: The park is easily accessible by road, making it a favored spot for both local and international visitors.

Visitor Management: Ayubia National Park is managed and maintained by the Wildlife Department of Khyber Pakhtunkhwa, with the aim of preserving the natural beauty and biodiversity of the area. The park is named after Muhammad Ayub Khan, the second President of Pakistan, who played a key role in its establishment in 1984.

Research and Monitoring: The management of the park focuses on conservation efforts, environmental education, and sustainable tourism. Trails and walking paths are maintained to allow visitors to explore the park while minimizing their impact on the ecosystem. Additionally, the Wildlife Department conducts regular surveys to monitor the health of the flora and fauna within the park.
