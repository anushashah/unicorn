Animal Wildlife

Sindh Ibex: This mountain goat species is found in the rocky hills of the park, navigating the steep cliffs with agility.

Caracal: Also known as the desert lynx, the caracal is a medium-sized wild cat with distinctive tufted ears. It can be spotted in the park's arid regions.

Indian Fox: The park is inhabited by the Indian fox, also known as the Bengal fox, which is well-adapted to the arid and semi-arid landscapes.

Leopard: Kirthar National Park is home to the elusive Persian leopard, a rare and endangered species. The rugged terrain provides suitable habitat for these big cats.

striped hyena

The striped hyena is a primarily nocturnal animal, which typically only leaves its den at the onset of total darkness, returning before sunrise.

The striped hyena is not considered very aggressive and usually avoids contact with other wildlife. Misunderstood and viewed as dangerous or destructive, it is poisoned, trapped, or shot for supposedly preying on livestock or raiding farms.

Indian Wolf

The Indian wolf (Canis lupus pallipes) is a subspecies of gray wolf that ranges from Southwest Asia to the Indian subcontinent.

There are approximately 400-1,100 wolves living in the Himalayan Region and 4,000-6,000 wolves in the Peninsular Region. Wolves in the Himalayan Region prefer colder, steeper habitats while those in the Peninsular Region are mostly found in agropastoral areas that have less forest density (approximately 280,400km²).

Sindh Ibex
