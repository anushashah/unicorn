Terrain and Topography of Hazarganji National Park

Location: Hazarganji National Park is situated about 20 kilometers southwest of Quetta, the provincial capital of Balochistan.

Altitude: The park's altitude varies, but it generally ranges from around 2,000 to 3,300 meters above sea level.

Landform: The park encompasses a variety of landforms, including rugged mountains, hills, and valleys. The landscape is characterized by rocky outcrops and steep slopes.

Geology: The region's geology is diverse, with different types of rocks and formations. The presence of limestone, shale, and sandstone contributes to the park's geological richness.

Flora: Hazarganji National Park is known for its unique vegetation, which includes a mix of juniper forests, shrubs, and grasses. The park is home to various plant species adapted to the arid and semi-arid climate of the region.

Fauna: The park is inhabited by a variety of wildlife species, including the Suleiman Markhor (a type of wild goat), ibex, urial sheep, chinkara gazelle, porcupines, and a diverse range of bird species. The park is particularly important for the conservation of the Suleiman Markhor.

Water Sources: The park is characterized by limited water sources. Springs and small streams may be present, providing crucial water supply for the park's wildlife.

Accessibility: Access to certain areas of the park may be challenging due to the rugged terrain. Some parts may be accessible only by foot or on horseback.

Conservation Significance: Hazarganji National Park is not only important for its biodiversity but also for its role in the conservation of endangered and endemic species, particularly the Suleiman Markhor.
