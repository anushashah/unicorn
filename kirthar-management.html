Management And National Park Location

Kirthar National Park is a protected area located in the Kirthar Mountain Range of Sindh, Pakistan. Established in 1974, it covers an expansive area of approximately 3,087 square kilometers, making it one of the largest national parks in the country. The park is renowned for its diverse landscapes, rich biodiversity, and cultural significance.

Administration: The park is managed by the Sindh Wildlife Department, with a dedicated team overseeing its day-to-day operations.The park is divided into zones for effective management, including core conservation areas and zones for sustainable recreational activities.

Conservation Efforts: Strict conservation measures are in place to protect endangered species such as the Sindh ibex and Urial sheep. Habitat preservation and restoration programs aim to maintain the ecological balance within the park.

Visitor Management: Controlled access and well-marked trails are implemented to minimize the impact of tourism on the ecosystem. Eco-friendly camping sites are designated to promote responsible tourism.

Research and Monitoring: Ongoing research programs focus on monitoring the park's flora and fauna, studying migration patterns, and assessing the impact of climate change. Satellite imagery and remote sensing technologies are employed for effective surveillance and management.

Community Involvement: Local communities are engaged in conservation efforts through education and awareness programs. Sustainable livelihood initiatives, such as eco-friendly handicrafts and guided tours, are encouraged to enhance local support for conservation.

Infrastructure Development: Adequate infrastructure, including well-maintained trails, visitor centers, and information boards, is in place to facilitate tourism while minimizing environmental impact.

Law Enforcement: A dedicated team of park rangers and wildlife enforcement officers ensures the enforcement of rules and regulations to prevent poaching and illegal activities within the park.
