Management And National Park Location

Executive Summary Hazarganji National Park, located in the province of Balochistan, is a precious natural reserve known for its diverse flora and fauna. The purpose of this management plan is to provide a comprehensive framework for the sustainable management and conservation of Hazarganji National Park. This plan outlines key strategies and actions to preserve biodiversity, enhance visitor experiences, and promote community engagement.

A. Flora and Fauna Protection: Implement and enforce strict anti-poaching measures Establish wildlife corridors to ensure genetic diversity Conduct regular surveys and studies on the park's biodiversity .

Visitor Management: Establish designated trails and viewing points to minimize human impact Implement carrying capacity limits to prevent overcrowding Develop educational programs to raise visitor awareness

Community Engagement and Empowerment: Collaborate with local communities for sustainable resource management Implement revenue-sharing mechanisms to benefit nearby residents Provide training and employment opportunities for community members.

Community Engagement: Involve local communities in park management decisions through participatory approaches. Develop sustainable livelihood programs for nearby communities to reduce dependence on park resources. Conduct educational programs to raise awareness about the park's ecological significance.

Research and Monitoring: Support and facilitate scientific research within the park Establish a monitoring system for key species Share research findings with the global scientific community .

Visitor Monitoring: Implement a system to monitor visitor numbers and behaviors Collect feedback for continuous improvement Adjust management strategies based on monitoring results.

Technology Integration: Utilize modern technology, such as satellite imagery and GIS, for monitoring and managing the park's resources. Implement an online reservation system for tourism activities to regulate visitor numbers.

Emergency Response and Disaster Management: Develop and regularly update emergency response plans to address natural disasters or unexpected events. Conduct regular drills and training for park staff to ensure preparedness in case of emergencies.

Financial Sustainability: Develop sustainable funding mechanisms, including entrance fees, grants, and partnerships with conservation organizations. Establish public-private partnerships for the development and maintenance of tourism infrastructure.
